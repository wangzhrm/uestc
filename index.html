Economic Insight

This simulation illustrates the fundamental problem in oligopolies: collusion maximizes total profit ($3,600 at 60 gallons), but individual incentives lead to higher production and lower prices.

When Jack and Jill compete, they typically end up in a Nash Equilibrium where each produces 40 gallons, resulting in a market price of $40 and combined profits of $3,200 - $400 less than the collusion scenario.

This demonstrates why oligopolies often try to coordinate output (though such coordination may be illegal in many jurisdictions).
